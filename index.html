About Me

Welcome! I’m a PhD student in Economics at the University of Wisconsin–Madison. My research lies at the intersection of development economics, labor markets, and gender inequality. I’m especially interested in how social norms and structural barriers shape individuals’ decisions, particularly for women in low-income contexts. I aim to use field experiments and experimental economics to study questions of discrimination and decision-making, with a focus on real-world mechanisms behind persistent inequalities.

Before starting my PhD, I completed a Master of Arts in Public Policy with a Certificate in Research Methods at the University of Chicago’s Harris School of Public Policy.
